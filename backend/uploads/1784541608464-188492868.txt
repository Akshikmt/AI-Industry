Equipment Tag: T-202

1. INCIDENT DETAILS
- Date Recorded: 2026-07-12
- Incident Class: Critical Alarm - Sensor Deviation
- Primary Investigator: Operator J. Miller

2. SUMMARY OF SPECIFICATIONS DEVIATION
During the scheduled emergency shutdown test on 2026-07-12, turbine deceleration curves registered a severe transient vibration spike. 
Rotor vibration levels peaked at 3.4 mm/s, exceeding the operational safety limit of 2.0 mm/s.

3. PROBABLE CAUSE ANALYSIS
A telemetry sweep indicates throttle valve misalignment. The steam governor valves are displaying asymmetric closure rates on deceleration. This throttling asymmetry triggers steam flow imbalances, causing mechanical rotor vibrations.

4. REMEDIATION INSTRUCTIONS
- REALIGN governor valve linkages and calibrate alignment sensors.
- RUN a static control loop check on the throttle stroke curves prior to restarting the main steam loop.
- INSPECT rotor blades for friction scratching if next start test vibration exceeds 1.8 mm/s.
